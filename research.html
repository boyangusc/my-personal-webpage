Publications

Publication
Information-seeking lobbying and strategic stockpiling under trade policy uncertainty
Strategic Management Journal

This study investigates how firms engage in information-seeking lobbying to address trade policy uncertainty. Using shipping records of U.S. firms during the 2018 U.S.–China trade war, the study finds that lobbying firms increased imports of soon-to-be-tariffed products before tariff lists were publicly released.

Further analysis shows that lobbying firms were less likely to request tariff exemptions for products they had preemptively stockpiled, suggesting that information-seeking lobbying during policy formulation may reduce the need for costly government engagement during implementation.

Publication
Navigating Geopolitical Risks: How U.S. Firms Adjust Supply Chains Amidst U.S.–China Rivalry
Journal of International Business Studies · with Jinyuan Song, Yifan Wei, and Jing Li

We investigate how U.S. firms adapt their supply chains in China in response to geopolitical risks from the U.S.–China rivalry. We offer a legitimacy-efficiency balancing perspective to understand firm decisions.

Analyzing firm-level supplier data from 2009 to 2022, we find that strategic-industry firms maintain fewer Chinese suppliers after 2017, while China dependence for revenue or supplies tempers this tendency.

Publication
China's Low-Productivity Innovation Drive: Evidence from Patents Production
Comparative Political Studies · with Yuen Yuen Ang, Nan Jia, and Kenneth G. Huang

Using 4.6 million Chinese patents from 1990 through 2014, this study examines how bureaucratic patent targets shaped the volume and novelty of innovation. The findings suggest that targets and political competition encouraged local governments to boost non-novel patents over novel patents.

Publication
Move to Success? Headquarters Relocation, Political Favoritism, and Corporate Performance
Journal of Corporate Finance · with Shuo Chen and Xun Yan

This study documents a corporate rent-seeking phenomenon in China: headquarters relocation to the political center. Firms relocating to Beijing enjoy increased political favors, but their productivity and innovation worsen after relocation, unlike firms moving to Shanghai or Shenzhen.
